Welcome to Phy-Hub

About Phy-Hub:

This site aims to provide an intuitive, visual, and comprehensive overview of physics topics, starting with the Handbook of Special Relativity for now, with more to be added later.

I went down a lot of rabbit holes and invested a lot of time digging through resources, including some very obscure books, to find the clearest explanations for every concept. I also added some of my own way of explaining and deriving the topics to make things less abstract and more approachable. I got intrigued enough in certain parts that I did some extra research of my own, and I’ve included the most interesting bits in their own sections.

Each topic starts with a non-mathematical overview designed for everyone, whether you’re a student or just curious. We’ll start from first principles and the foundational experiments and build from there. I will aim to maintain as much simplicity as I can and avoid unnecessary abstract ways to explain concepts, teaching things visually through static and interactive diagrams, and relatable real-world analogies to build a solid foundational understanding first, before we move onto the math.

The rest will focus on the derivations of all the core concepts, always using the simplest possible mathematics to do so. From here, we’ll explore some interesting topics you won't find in most textbooks, including a few unique sections that you’ll only find here.

Beyond the content on Phy-Hub, I also plan to point you towards other high-quality resources that I personally believe are worth learning the topics from.

While physics is my thing, writing is not, so some parts might read a little rough, for now anyhow. If any sections feel confusing or if you spot a mistake, please feel free to let me know by leaving some feedback. The content pages will have a suggestion box link in the bottom right of the page, or if you wish to send me a message now, click the message link at the bottom of this page.

About Me:

Hi, I’m Phyl Hubble, or as my friends sometimes call me, a \( \frac{d^3x}{dt^3} \).

In my early teens, I was torn between pursuing physics or zoology, but that quickly narrowed to physics due to dyslexia getting in the way of any subject that required better reading and writing skill than I had at the time, while physics and math was so much simpler to process for me.

I focused on math and physics through school and took a four-year Master’s in Theoretical Physics. I then started a PhD, but like many students, I found the reality didn't match the original pitch. Realizing the program wouldn't lead me toward my goals, and feeling more like a frontend developer than a physicist, I decided to leave. However, I’ve since put those coding skills to good use, including building this website.

My objective never changed, I am still driven to understand physics better, with the naive hope to one day contribute to the foundations of physics.
